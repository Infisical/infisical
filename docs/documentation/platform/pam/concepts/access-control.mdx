Access control in PAM happens at two levels. First, someone needs to be added to PAM itself — that's **product membership**. Then, they need access to specific folders or accounts — that's **resource membership**.

## Product Membership

Before anyone can do anything in PAM, they need to be added as a product member. This is the "entry ticket" to PAM.

There are two product roles:

| Role | What they see |
|------|---------------|
| **Product Admin** | Everything — all folders, all accounts, all settings |
| **Product Member** | Only folders and accounts they've been granted access to |

Product Admins set up the structure (create folders, create templates) and decide who gets access to what. Product Members only see what they've been explicitly granted.

<Note>
Organization Admins can always access PAM as Product Admins without being explicitly added.
</Note>

### Adding Product Members

1. Go to **Privileged Access Management → Access Control**
2. Click **Add Member**
3. Select a user or group
4. Choose **Product Admin** or **Product Member**
5. Click **Add**

## Resource Membership

Once someone is a product member, they still need access to specific folders or accounts. This is done through **resource memberships** — assigning them a role on a folder or account.

### Roles

| Role | What they can do |
|------|------------------|
| **Admin** | Full control — create/edit/delete accounts, manage memberships, connect, view all sessions |
| **Connector** | Connect to accounts, view their own sessions |
| **Requester** | Request access to accounts |
| **Auditor** | View all sessions and logs (read-only) |

### Folder vs Account Memberships

You can assign roles at two levels:

**Folder memberships** apply to all accounts in the folder. This is the primary way to grant access — if someone needs access to a set of accounts, grant them a role on the folder.

**Account memberships** apply to a single account. Use this for exceptions — a contractor who needs just one database, or temporary elevated access to a specific account.

### How to Assign Memberships

For folders:
1. Open the folder and go to **Memberships**
2. Click **Add Member**
3. Select user/group, role, and optional expiration
4. Click **Add**

For accounts:
1. Open the account and go to **Memberships**
2. Same process as above

## Putting It Together

Here's a typical setup:

1. **Product Admin** adds the Platform team lead as a Product Member
2. **Product Admin** creates a `prod-databases` folder
3. **Product Admin** grants the team lead **Admin** on that folder
4. **Team lead** (now a folder Admin) adds accounts to the folder
5. **Team lead** grants the rest of the team **Connector** on the folder
6. **Team members** can now connect to any account in the folder

The Product Admin sets up the structure. The folder Admin manages day-to-day access within their scope. Team members connect to what they need.
